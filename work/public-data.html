Public Data

If you've ever had to research public records, you know the story is almost always the same: while they are technically available to the public, they sure aren't very actionable. I've spent much of the past few years attempting to help remedy this problem using the technological wonders of the modern web. Here are the highlights.

Vigilant

In 2015, along with Mike Phillips and Zac Palin, I co-founded Vigilant, a comprehensive public records search and monitoring platform. I conceived and built the initial core technologies behind the platform, and structured much of the data ingestion across what has become thousands of sources. I also handled design for both UI / UX and branding / marketing. Vigilant's first customer was the Clinton presidential campaign, and has since been employed regularly by prominent organizations in politics, finance, and journalism.

California Lobby Search

In 2018, I built and launched California Lobby Search, an open source research tool that lets users view California state lobbying activity by bill or keyword. I did this mainly because as a California resident it bugged the crap out of me that you couldn't do this already.
